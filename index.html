Introduction to Blood Cells

Red Blood Cells (RBCs) Function:

  • Oxygen transport

Normal morphology:

  • Biconcave shape

Common Abnormalities:

  • Anisocytosis (size variation)
  • Poikilocytosis (shape variation)

Mnemonic: "Oxygen Ovals"

  • RBCs are the ovals that carry oxygen

RBCs and Their Abnormalities

Cabot Ring

  • Refer to remnants of the mitotic spindle within reticulocytes that form delicate, ring-like or figure-of-eight structures

  • Found in Hemolytic Anemia

Howell-jolly Body

  • Howell-Jolly bodies appear in peripheral blood smears in patients with absent or deficient spleen function

  • They are pathognomonic for splenic dysfunction

Hemoglobin C

  • Hemoglobin C crystals exist in the peripheral blood smear of a patient diagnosed with Hemoglobin C disease

  • These crystals are dense rectangular structures composed of precipitated hemoglobin C

Pappenheimer bodies

  • Pappenheimer bodies in the presence of microcytic anemia suggest sideroblastic erythropoiesis, which may be seen in sideroblastic anemia, lead poisoning, or hemochromatosis

  • Surgical asplenia may result in the formation of Pappenheimer bodies

Platelets

Function:

Blood Clotting

Normal appearance:

Small, irregular fragments

"Tiny Clotters":

Platelets are tiny cellular fragments (derived from Megakaryocytes) that help in clotting
